Design hypotheses

Screener

In UserZoom testing, 10% of users dropped off on the start page citing a lack of NHS Number or confusion around if their contact details are shared with the NHS.

On further pages, similar reasons were cited by users as reasons to drop out of the journey.

If we provide users with screener questions
Then users will better understand the service requirements
Because users will be provided with specific information on each requirement

If we provide users with screener questions
Then users will understand the NHS number requirement better
Because users will be provided with specific information about how to find their NHS Number, and what it is used for

If we provide users with screener questions
Then users will understand the contact details requirement better
Because users will be provided with specific information about what types of details the NHS holds and interactions with which services will likely mean their details are saved

Confirm your details interstitial

In user labs and UserZoom testing, users were left confused at what they were being asked of at the "confirm your identity" page.

They didn't understand the following steps of the service nor why they were being asked to submit personal identifiable data.

If we provide an easy to read summary before the "your details" form
Then users will know what they will need to do to authenticate themselves for the service
Because the stages will be shown clearly to them before they start

If we provide an easy to read summary before the "your details" form
Then users will better understand why they are being asked for personal data
Because it will be clearer why that data is being used and what role it plays in verifying the user

Preference question interstitial

Users are required to remember many new details about the NHS and how their data is used, and undergo an authentication process, before they can set their preference.

If we provide users with a summary of how their data is used by the NHS and how the process works
Then users will be better placed to make an informed decision
Because the need to remember every detail relevant to the opt-out service will be reduced
