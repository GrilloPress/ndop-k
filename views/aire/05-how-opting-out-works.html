If you opt-out of sharing your personally identifiable data, details like your name and address will no longer appear in data shared by the NHS. This will only happen if opt-outs apply to the data set being shared. There are some circumstances where opt-outs do not apply, and your personally identifiable data will still be used.

The opt-out process

  1. You set an opt-out preference
  2. Our automated IT systems process this information
  3. We produce a record of your opt-out information
  4. We update our lists of current opt-out preferences

The record of your opt-out information allows the NHS to check the date your opt-out was received. It also shows if an opt-out decision has been withdrawn. This tells us when we are able to share personally identifiable data and when we need to apply an opt-out preference.

Once an opt-out request is received, it can take up to 21 days before it can be applied to future data releases. Once applied, an opt-out will continue to be upheld following a patient's death.

Where opt-outs do not apply

This opt-out will not apply:

  • Where the information is used for purposes relating to your individual care
  • Where the personal confidential information does not contain your NHS number
  • If you have given consent for your data to be used for a specific reason, like a medical research study
  • Where data is anonymised which means individuals are not identifiable from the information
  • Where there is a legal requirement to provide information, such as an order of court
  • Where there is an overriding public interest in the disclosure of your data
  • Where information is used to support the management of communicable diseases and other risks to public health
  • Where patient registration information is supplied to the Office for National Statistics
